Accessibility Statement

Bolidesh Foundation is dedicated to making our website, bolidesh.in, accessible to all users, including those with disabilities, ensuring an inclusive and equitable experience in line with our mission to preserve cultural heritage and empower communities.

1. Our Commitment to Accessibility

At Bolidesh Foundation, we believe that access to cultural heritage and community resources should be universal. Our Accessibility Statement reflects our commitment to ensuring that bolidesh.in is usable by everyone, regardless of physical, sensory, cognitive, or technological limitations. We strive to meet or exceed the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards, which provide a framework for creating accessible digital content. Our goal is to foster an inclusive online environment where all users can engage with our cultural archives, educational resources, and community initiatives.

This commitment aligns with our mission to preserve and share cultural heritage, ensuring that individuals with disabilities—whether visual, auditory, motor, or cognitive—can fully participate in our digital offerings. We continuously work to improve accessibility and welcome feedback to enhance the user experience.

2. Accessibility Features

Bolidesh Foundation has implemented a range of accessibility features to ensure that bolidesh.in is navigable, understandable, and usable for all users. These features include:

  • Keyboard Navigation: All interactive elements, including menus, links, forms, and buttons, are fully accessible via keyboard, allowing users with motor disabilities or those who rely on keyboard input to navigate seamlessly.
  • Screen Reader Compatibility: The website uses semantic HTML, ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) landmarks, and descriptive labels to ensure compatibility with screen readers like NVDA, JAWS, and VoiceOver.
  • Color Contrast: Text and background elements meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA contrast ratios (minimum 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text) to enhance readability for users with visual impairments.
  • Alternative Text: All meaningful images, icons, and graphics include descriptive alt text to provide context for screen reader users and those with visual impairments.
  • Resizable Text: Users can adjust font sizes using browser settings or assistive technologies without disrupting the website’s layout or functionality.
  • Accessible Forms: Forms include clear labels, instructions, and error messages that are compatible with assistive technologies, ensuring users can complete actions like newsletter subscriptions or donations.
  • Responsive Design: The website is optimized for various devices, including desktops, tablets, and mobile phones, with layouts that adapt to different screen sizes and resolutions.
  • Video and Multimedia Accessibility: Videos include captions, transcripts, and audio descriptions where feasible, supporting users with hearing or visual impairments.
  • Consistent Navigation: The website maintains a predictable structure and navigation pattern to assist users with cognitive disabilities.

3. Accessibility Standards and Compliance

Bolidesh Foundation is committed to adhering to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA, which are internationally recognized standards for web accessibility. These guidelines cover perceivability, operability, understandability, and robustness, ensuring that digital content is accessible to a wide range of users. We also strive to comply with India’s accessibility regulations, such as the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, and other applicable standards.

Our accessibility efforts are guided by the following WCAG principles:

  • Perceivable: Information and user interface components must be presentable in ways users can perceive (e.g., alt text, captions).
  • Operable: User interface components and navigation must be operable by all users (e.g., keyboard accessibility).
  • Understandable: Information and operation of the user interface must be clear and intuitive.
  • Robust: Content must be compatible with current and future user tools, including assistive technologies.

4. Current Limitations

While we aim to provide a fully accessible experience, certain limitations may exist due to:

  • Third-Party Content: Embedded content, such as social media widgets or external media, may not fully comply with WCAG standards, as we have limited control over third-party platforms.
  • Archived Materials: Older content, such as historical documents or legacy media, may lack accessibility features like captions or alt text due to their original format.
  • Technical Constraints: Some advanced features, such as interactive cultural exhibits, may have partial accessibility limitations during development.

We are actively working to address these limitations by retrofitting older content, collaborating with third-party providers, and prioritizing accessibility in new developments.

5. Ongoing Accessibility Efforts

Bolidesh Foundation is committed to continuous improvement in web accessibility. Our ongoing efforts include:

  • Regular Audits: Conducting accessibility audits using automated tools and manual testing to identify and resolve barriers.
  • Staff Training: Providing training to content creators, developers, and staff on WCAG guidelines and inclusive design principles.
  • User Testing: Engaging users with disabilities to test the website and provide feedback on usability.
  • Content Updates: Ensuring all new content, including articles, videos, and forms, adheres to accessibility standards.
  • Technology Integration: Exploring tools like text-to-speech, high-contrast modes, and customizable interfaces to enhance accessibility.

7. Compatibility with Assistive Technologies

Bolidesh.in is designed to be compatible with modern web browsers (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge) and assistive technologies, including:

  • Screen Readers: NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, and TalkBack.
  • Screen Magnifiers: ZoomText and browser-based zoom functions.
  • Voice Control Software: Dragon NaturallySpeaking and built-in voice assistants.
  • Alternative Input Devices: Keyboard-only navigation and switch devices.

We test the website across these tools to ensure compatibility, but we encourage users to report any issues for further improvement.

8. Accessibility for Cultural Content

As a cultural heritage organization, we recognize the importance of making our archives, historical content, and educational resources accessible. We are implementing features like accessible digital archives, captioned videos, and simplified text options to ensure that users with disabilities can engage with our cultural materials.

9. Legal and Compliance

Bolidesh Foundation is committed to complying with applicable accessibility laws, including India’s Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, and international standards like WCAG 2.1. While this Accessibility Statement outlines our goals, it does not alter our legal obligations under our Terms of Service or other policies.
