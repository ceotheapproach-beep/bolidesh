Violence in Uttar Pradesh Over 'I Love Muhammad' Campaign: Clashes in Bareilly, Maulana Tauqeer Raza Khan Among Eight Arrested (27 September 2025)

27 September 2025 Bareilly, Uttar Pradesh National / Politics

On 27 September 2025, Bareilly in Uttar Pradesh descended into tension and violence as a protest supporting the global 'I Love Muhammad' campaign turned chaotic, leading to clashes between demonstrators and police. Authorities arrested eight individuals, including influential cleric Maulana Tauqeer Raza Khan, chief of the Ittehad-e-Millat Council, for inciting the unrest. 0 1 2 The incident highlights how international religious controversies are manifesting locally, challenging law and order in sensitive regions like Uttar Pradesh. 3

Understanding the 'I Love Muhammad' Campaign

The campaign emerged globally as a response to perceived insults against Prophet Muhammad, gaining traction on social media among Muslim communities expressing faith and solidarity. 8 In India, it has sparked public demonstrations and posts, particularly in areas with strong religious influences. Bareilly, known for its religious diversity and the prominence of the Ahle Sunnat wal Jamaat (Barelvi) sect, became a focal point. Tauqeer Raza Khan, a politically active cleric and descendant of Barelvi founder Ahmed Raza Khan, organized the protest against alleged derogatory remarks in places like Shahjahanpur and police actions in Kanpur during Eid-e-Milad-un-Nabi. 0 7

Timeline of the Incident: From Peaceful March to Clashes

The demonstration began as a planned march after Friday prayers on September 26, intended to gather at Islamia Ground and proceed to a main square. 6 Police had permitted only a limited gathering but imposed Section 163 of the BNSS (banning unauthorized assemblies) and Section 144 to prevent escalation. 1 When the crowd, exceeding limits, reached a commercial area, barricades were erected. Khan's inflammatory speech allegedly incited the mob to breach them, leading to stone-pelting on police and vandalism. 4 5 Officers responded with lathi charges and tear gas, injuring several personnel mildly. 2 Khan was initially under house arrest but arrested late Friday night along with seven associates, sent to 14-day judicial custody. 3

Tauqeer Raza Khan's Role and Political Ramifications

Khan's arrest elevates the incident beyond law enforcement, into religious and political territory. As a key figure with deep influence in Bareilly's Muslim community, his detention—linked to seven FIRs—has sparked outrage, with crowds protesting outside his residence. 5 2 Known for criticizing government policies on minority rights, his involvement signals a crackdown on opposition-aligned religious gatherings. 4 Uttar Pradesh's administration, under Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath, has vowed strict action against disruptors, emphasizing no tolerance for unrest. 4

Global Context and National Impact

The clashes underscore ongoing debates on religious freedom in India, drawing international scrutiny from human rights groups over police responses to protests. 8 Locally, it risks deepening communal polarization, with Hindu nationalist groups supporting the crackdown as rule of law, while minorities view it as suppression. 7 Over 36 detentions and 11 FIRs naming 2,000+ individuals highlight the scale. 1

The Path Forward: Balancing Sensitivity and Order

Bareilly remains tense but controlled, with additional police and Rapid Action Force deployments. Internet suspension aims to curb rumors, while authorities monitor for retaliation. 1 The state faces the challenge of allowing faith expressions without compromising public peace, especially with Khan's arrest potentially fueling further unrest ahead of elections. 3
