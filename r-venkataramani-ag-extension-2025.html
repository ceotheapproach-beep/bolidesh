Supreme Guardian of Law: Senior Advocate R. Venkataramani's Unprecedented Two-Year Extension as India's Attorney General (27 September 2025)

27 September 2025 New Delhi, India National / Politics

The Central Government has re-appointed Senior Advocate R. Venkataramani as the Attorney General for India for a further two years, effective from October 1, 2025. 0 2 3 This extension, notified by the Ministry of Law and Justice, underscores the government's confidence in his expertise amid sensitive constitutional matters, ensuring continuity in the highest legal office under Article 76 of the Constitution. 4 5

R. Venkataramani: A Profile and Legal Legacy

Born on April 13, 1950, R. Venkataramani is a veteran constitutional lawyer with over four decades of experience. 1 He earned his law degree from Madras and began his career in Delhi, working under renowned jurist P.P. Rao in the late 1970s. 1 Designated a Senior Advocate by the Supreme Court in 1997, he served as a Member of the Law Commission in 2010. 1 Appointed AG in October 2022 succeeding K.K. Venugopal, he has handled high-profile cases with a calm, reasoned approach, balancing advocacy and judicial respect. 1 7

Timing and Significance of the Re-Appointment: Need for Stability

This extension is crucial in a landscape of landmark constitutional cases, providing continuity where the AG's familiarity is vital. 4

1. Continuity in Major Cases: Pending matters like Article 370 challenges, electoral bonds, Aadhaar privacy, and fundamental rights require seasoned representation. 7 Venkataramani's presence avoids disruptions in these complex litigations.

2. Government's Vote of Confidence: Traditionally tied to government tenure, this renewal signals full satisfaction with his defense of the government's position while upholding judicial dignity. 7

3. Advancing Legal Reforms: He will collaborate on reforms like criminal law amendments and tribunal streamlining. 7

Global Perspective: Judicial-Government Interface

The AG role parallels the UK's Attorney General and US Solicitor General but is uniquely Indian, advising on policy while representing the government. 1 Venkataramani's re-appointment affirms his ability to navigate this balance, offering opinions independent of political pressure. 7

Challenges and Future Role

Over the next two years, Venkataramani will face tests in constitutional interpretation, center-state relations, and managing judicial activism. 7 His diplomatic skills will be key in bridging executive and judiciary amid ideological tensions.
