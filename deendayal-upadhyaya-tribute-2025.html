PM Modi Pays Tribute to Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya on His Birth Anniversary (26 September 2025)

26 September 2025 New Delhi, India Politics / Governance

On 26 September 2025, India commemorated the birth anniversary of Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya, co-founder and key ideologue of the Bharatiya Jana Sangh. Prime Minister Narendra Modi paid tribute to Upadhyaya, describing him as not only a great organizer but also a guiding figure and source of inspiration for Indian politics and social philosophy.

Deendayal Upadhyaya: Introduction and Ideological Legacy

Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya (25 September 1916 – 11 February 1968) had a profound influence on the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) and its parent organization, the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS).

1. Integral Humanism

  • Core Principle: Views humans as integrated entities of body, mind, intellect, and soul, not merely economic or material units.
  • Society: Emphasizes harmony between the individual and society; neither dominates the other.
  • Development Goal: Rejects both Western capitalism and communism, advocating a duty-based, human-centered model of development.
  • Concept of Antyodaya: Focuses on the "upliftment of the last person" in society, guiding government policies to ensure welfare for the poorest and most marginalized.

2. Organizer and Leader

Upadhyaya served as General Secretary of the Bharatiya Jana Sangh from 1951 to 1967 and later became its President, strengthening the organization at the grassroots level.

PM Modi’s Tribute and Message

Modi highlighted Upadhyaya’s ideological and political impact, describing his life and thoughts as "guiding principles" for the current government.

1. Guiding Principle

  • Authenticity: Welfare schemes such as free food, housing, and healthcare for the poor are directly inspired by Upadhyaya’s Antyodaya principles.
  • National Identity: Emphasis on cultural nationalism and national pride.

2. Antyodaya and Government Policies

Deendayal Upadhyaya Principle Current Government Schemes / Policy Link
Antyodaya (upliftment of society’s last person) Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana: Free ration distribution for the poor
Health and Nutrition Ayushman Bharat Scheme: Health insurance coverage for the most vulnerable
Rural Development and Housing Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana: Providing permanent housing to all
Economic Democracy Jan Dhan Yojana and Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT): Direct transfer of government benefits, reducing corruption

National Discourse and Political Implications

  • Ideological Anchoring: BJP and RSS emphasize Upadhyaya, Shyama Prasad Mukherjee, and Vallabhbhai Patel as part of their ideological heritage. Modi’s tribute reinforces that Integral Humanism remains the core philosophy, distinct from Nehruvian socialism or Western liberalism.
  • Political Signal: The tribute inspires party workers and communicates that government policies are not only materialistic but rooted in philosophy and Indian cultural values.
  • Education and Public Sector Influence: Several institutions, schemes, and public places have been named after Upadhyaya to preserve his legacy for future generations.

Conclusion

The tribute by Prime Minister Narendra Modi on 26 September 2025 was more than ceremonial. It reaffirmed the government’s commitment to policies influenced by Upadhyaya’s Integral Humanism and Antyodaya philosophy, promoting a spiritually enriched, economically self-reliant, and culturally strong India.
