Humanitarian Crisis and Diplomatic Pressure: India Demands Immediate Release of 27 More Indian Citizens from War-Torn Areas in Russia (27 September 2025)

27 September 2025 New Delhi, India National / International

On 27 September 2025, the Indian government held high-level diplomatic talks with Moscow, demanding the immediate and safe release of 27 additional Indian nationals recently recruited into the Russian military and deployed to the Ukraine frontlines. 1 8 Initially lured with promises of high-paying support roles, these youths from rural and semi-urban areas were deceived into combat positions, highlighting a severe humanitarian crisis and potential violations of international labor laws. 0 2

The Recruitment Trap: From 'Support Staff' to Combatant

At the heart of this issue is a human trafficking network targeting unemployed Indian youth, promising lucrative jobs in Russia as construction workers, cooks, or cleaners in non-combat military units, with salaries of $1,500–2,000 monthly. 9 3 Routed via Dubai or Central Asia, victims were coerced into signing Russian-language contracts upon arrival, followed by basic training and frontline deployment in Ukraine against their will. 2 10 Desperate video messages to families reveal threats of execution for escape attempts. 3

Diplomatic Pressure and India's Response

India has treated this as a sensitive diplomatic challenge straining ties with ally Russia. 3 The Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) engaged Russian officials in Delhi and Moscow, securing some releases but uncovering the issue's scale. 0 MEA spokesperson Randhir Jaiswal stated: "We have conveyed a firm message to Russia's Foreign and Defense Ministries that Indian citizens must not be sent to conflict zones." 1 India highlighted potential breaches of ILO conventions and anti-trafficking protocols. 6

Deaths and Family Trauma

The crisis's tragedy includes at least 12 confirmed Indian deaths in the conflict, drawing national focus. 1 8 Families endure grief and financial ruin from loans taken to fund the 'jobs,' demanding repatriation and agent prosecutions. 4

Crackdown on Agents: Dismantling the Trafficking Network

The CBI has registered cases against the network active in states like Punjab, Haryana, Kerala, and Uttar Pradesh, arresting key agents using social media for deception. 13 International branches remain a challenge. 9

Diplomatic Ties and Ethical Responsibilities

This strains the India-Russia strategic partnership, with India balancing citizen protection and relations. 4 Focus is on immediate releases and assurances against future recruitment. 5 It underscores India's duty to safeguard citizens from foreign conflicts via stricter enforcement. 7
