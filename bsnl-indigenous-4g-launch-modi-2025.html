Make in India’s Telecom Moment: PM Modi Launches BSNL’s Indigenous 4G Stack – India Breaks Global Cartel (27 September 2025)

27 September 2025 New Delhi, India National / Technology

On 27 September 2025, Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited’s (BSNL) fully indigenous 4G telecom stack from Jharsuguda, Odisha, marking a pivotal moment in India’s technological sovereignty. This launch not only revives BSNL but also positions India among an elite group of nations—including China, Denmark, Sweden, and South Korea—capable of designing, manufacturing, and deploying complex wireless communication equipment, breaking the global cartel of foreign vendors. 0 2

What is the Indigenous 4G Stack and Why Does It Matter?

The Swadeshi 4G stack comprises a complete set of hardware and software components essential for operating a wireless network. Key elements include the core network for data routing and authentication, the radio access network (RAN) with base stations for signal transmission, and deployment management software. Developed by a consortium led by Tata Consultancy Services (TCS), the Centre for Development of Telematics (C-DOT), and Tejas Networks, this stack embodies the Atmanirbhar Bharat vision. 8

Strategically, telecom networks form a nation’s nervous system, supporting not just calls and internet but also critical infrastructure like power grids and defense communications. Reliance on foreign equipment, particularly from Chinese firms like Huawei and ZTE, posed security risks due to potential backdoors. The indigenous stack eliminates these vulnerabilities, ensuring digital sovereignty. 1

Table 1: Components of BSNL’s Indigenous 4G Stack
Component Description Developer
Core Network Handles data routing, authentication, and billing C-DOT
Radio Access Network (RAN) Base stations and towers for wireless signals Tejas Networks
Integration & Management Software Configures and manages the entire network TCS

BSNL’s Revival: A New Chapter in Telecom

Once a telecom giant, BSNL has struggled with losses and technological gaps against private rivals like Airtel and Jio. The indigenous 4G stack offers a lifeline, granting control over costs and upgrades for competitive pricing and rapid expansion, especially in rural areas. 3 The launch commissions over 97,500 solar-powered 4G towers at a cost of ₹37,000 crore, connecting 26,700 unserved villages—including remote and border regions—and benefiting 20 lakh new subscribers. 4 5 This cloud-based, 5G-ready network paves the way for future upgrades. 8

Economic and Technological Implications

This project unlocks vast opportunities for India’s tech sector. Built at lower costs than foreign alternatives, it saves billions in imports while creating high-skilled jobs for thousands of engineers. 6 TCS and C-DOT plan exports to the Global South, transforming India from a services provider to a hardware manufacturer and enhancing geopolitical influence through cost-effective, secure solutions. 9

Table 2: Key Milestones of the Launch
Milestone Details Impact
Towers Commissioned 97,500 (including 92,600 new 4G sites) ₹37,000 crore investment; 100% 4G saturation
Villages Connected 26,700 unserved villages Bridges digital divide in rural/border areas
Future Readiness 5G upgradable via software Positions India for 6G by 2030

Challenges and the Path Forward

Despite successes in controlled tests, challenges remain in scalability, performance matching global standards, and encouraging private sector adoption. Competing with veterans like Nokia and Ericsson requires sustained innovation. Overall, this launch symbolizes India’s confidence and strategic foresight, evolving it into a hardware and communications powerhouse. 7
