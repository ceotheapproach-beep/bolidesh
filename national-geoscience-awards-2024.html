National Geoscience Awards 2024 Distributed in New Delhi (26 September 2025)

26 September 2025 New Delhi, India

On 26 September 2025, the National Geoscience Awards 2024 were distributed in New Delhi during a grand ceremony. The awards event was organized in coordination with the Ministry of Earth Sciences and the Ministry of Mines, aiming to honor scientists, researchers, and organizations for their outstanding contributions in geology, mining, and related fields. This program highlights the growing importance of geological research for India’s mineral wealth and environmental security.

Significance of the National Geoscience Awards

The National Geoscience Awards (formerly known as the National Mineral Awards) are among the highest recognitions in India for geological research and exploration. Established in 1966, the awards acknowledge individuals and teams who have made notable contributions in:

  • Mineral Exploration: Discovery of new mineral deposits crucial for the country.
  • Geological Mapping: Development of high-resolution geological maps.
  • Geo-Environmental Studies: Monitoring natural hazards (earthquakes, landslides) and assessing the geological impacts of climate change.
  • Applied Geosciences: Practical applications in mining, water resources, and engineering geology.

These awards play a central role in promoting the exploration and development of critical minerals necessary for making India self-reliant.

Key Winners and Achievements of 2024

The ceremony presented awards in various categories, reflecting the diversity of geological research in India. Categories included the National Geoscience Award for Lifetime Achievement, the Main National Geoscience Award, and awards in specialized sub-disciplines.

1. Lifetime Achievement Award

This year’s award recognized a distinguished scientist for decades of exceptional contributions to geological research. The honor is typically awarded to those who have fundamentally contributed to India’s geological surveys and mineral policy development.

  • Contribution Highlights: Work related to understanding India’s ancient cratons and geological history, or developing innovative techniques for water resource management.

2. Main National Geoscience Award

This award recognizes individuals or teams whose work in the past three years has had significant and immediate impact in any field of geology.

  • Major Discoveries: Researchers were acknowledged for identifying new and untapped deposits of lithium, cobalt, and graphite, essential for India’s electric vehicle (EV) and renewable energy ambitions.

3. Awards in Sub-Disciplines

Sub-Discipline Awards and National Relevance
Sub-Discipline Winner’s Contribution (Example) National Importance
Mineral Exploration Development of novel geochemical techniques for identifying Rare Earth Elements. Ensures domestic supply of raw materials for future high-tech industries.
Applied Geosciences Advanced modeling and sensing systems for predicting large landslides in Himalayan regions. Safety of infrastructure and human life in mountainous areas.
Geo-Environmental Studies Research on groundwater recharge and water quality management, especially in arsenic-affected areas. Enhances water security and public health.
Marine Geology Exploration of polymetallic nodules in the Indian Ocean. Supports deep-sea mineral resource development and strengthens the maritime economy.

Address by Central Ministers and Policy Direction

  • Self-Reliance Emphasis: Ministers highlighted that the work of geoscientists forms the foundation of ‘Atmanirbhar Bharat’ and stressed the need to focus on India’s mineral wealth to reduce dependence on countries like China.
  • Technology and Innovation: Commitment to promote innovations in geological mapping and mineral exploration using drone surveys, AI, and machine learning.
  • Sustainable Mining: Importance of environmentally friendly and socially responsible mining practices to preserve ecological balance while developing minerals.

Future of Geological Research: Significance and Challenges

Significance:

  • Energy Transition: Domestic supply of lithium, nickel, and copper is critical as India moves from fossil fuels to clean energy.
  • Infrastructure: Accurate application of engineering geology is essential for constructing roads, tunnels, and dams, especially in unstable Himalayan regions.

Challenges:

  • Skilled Workforce: Shortage of trained personnel in advanced geological exploration techniques.
  • Data Integration: Need to integrate geological, geophysical, and remote sensing data for precise modeling.
  • Research Funding: Enhancing funding for geological research and exploration to meet international standards.

Conclusion

The distribution of the National Geoscience Awards 2024 on 26 September 2025 reflects India’s growing commitment to mineral security and geological innovation. These awards not only recognize individual achievements but also inspire the scientific community to address the country’s economic and environmental challenges. Aligning with Prime Minister Modi’s vision of self-reliance, the awards highlight the role of geoscientists in uncovering Earth’s hidden wealth and shaping the nation’s future. The ceremony sends a clear message: India is on the path to becoming a mining and geoscience superpower by promoting scientific research and innovation.
