Real Estate 'Fraud Network' Dismantled: CBI Raids Kolkata, Bengaluru, and Mumbai; Six Cases Registered Against Builders Duping Thousands (27 September 2025)

27 September 2025 New Delhi, India National / Business

On 27 September 2025, the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) conducted coordinated raids at 12 locations across Kolkata, Bengaluru, and Mumbai, exposing an organized real estate fraud network that defrauded thousands of homebuyers. 0 1 3 Following Supreme Court directives, the agency registered six FIRs against builders and unknown financial institution officials, targeting collusion through deceptive "subvention schemes" in home loans. 5 13 This probe highlights deep-rooted corruption in India's real estate sector, where middle-class savings are at risk due to regulatory gaps. 6

How the 'Fraud Network' Operated

The cases focus on three primary fraud methods systematically employed by the builders. First, phantom projects involved launching unapproved developments on disputed land, marketed aggressively with brochures promising non-existent amenities. 0 Buyers paid crores in installments, only for projects to vanish, leaving investors destitute. 3

Second, fund diversion— the most common tactic—saw collected payments redirected to unrelated ventures like stocks or films, or used in Ponzi-like schemes to finance new projects. 1 Delays ensued, followed by bankruptcy claims or legal entanglements when refunds were demanded. 5

Third, substandard construction used inferior materials, omitting promised facilities like pools or gyms, while legal hurdles delayed occupancy certificates, stranding buyers. 13 The "subvention scheme" exacerbated this by shifting EMIs to builders initially, only for defaults to burden buyers. 10

Table 1: Key Fraud Methods in Real Estate Scams
Method Description Impact on Buyers
Phantom Projects Unapproved launches on disputed land with false marketing Loss of initial payments; abandoned dreams
Fund Diversion Money redirected to unrelated uses or Ponzi financing Project delays; ongoing loan burdens
Substandard Construction Inferior materials; omitted amenities; legal delays Unsafe homes; denied possession

Why These Three Cities? The Multi-State Probe

The raids underscore the interstate nature of the crime. In Kolkata, links to chit fund scams lured middle-class savings into fake housing. 6 Bengaluru's IT boom saw delays and encroachments on government land with official collusion. 3 Mumbai exploited slum redevelopment for premium profits, shortchanging beneficiaries. 13 Seized documents and digital evidence will prove the nexus. 8 Named builders include Ithaca Estate Pvt Ltd, LGCL Urban Homes, Ozone Urbana, Shashwati Realty, ACME Realities, and MKHS Housing LLP. 3

Victims' Plight: Shattered Dreams and Lasting Trauma

Victims, mostly salaried middle-class families, NRIs, and retirees, face massive losses from home loans and EMIs without possession. 0 Psychological toll includes depression and social stigma, derailing retirement plans. 10

Regulatory Failures: Questioning RERA's Role

The scams question the Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA)'s effectiveness since 2016, with weak enforcement and official complicity allowing diversions. 1 The probe may extend to banks and civic bodies. 6

Conclusion

This multi-city operation marks a significant push against real estate fraud, protecting millions' "dream homes." 8 Success depends on rooting out structural corruption for future buyer safety. 15
