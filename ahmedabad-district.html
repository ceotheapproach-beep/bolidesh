About Ahmedabad District

Ahmedabad district, located in central Gujarat, India, is known for its bustling urban center, textile and pharmaceutical industries, historical architecture from the Sultanate era, and as the home of Mahatma Gandhi's Sabarmati Ashram. It features the Sabarmati River, fertile plains, and a mix of modern infrastructure with cultural heritage.

History

The city was founded in 1411 by Sultan Ahmed Shah of the Gujarat Sultanate as its capital, built next to the older Hindu town of Asawal. It grew as a trade hub under Muslim rulers, prospered during Mughal times, and became a center for textile manufacturing known as the "Manchester of the East." Under British rule, it had a military cantonment and was key in the independence movement with Gandhi's ashram. Post-independence, it was Gujarat's capital until 1970. The district's history includes ancient settlements from the 8th century with Bhil tribes, Solanki rule in the 11th century, and medieval developments. Geography

Ahmedabad is located on the banks of the Sabarmati River at 23°02′N 72°35′E, spanning 8,107 km² in central Gujarat. The district features flat plains with some sandy areas, elevation around 53 meters, and is part of the Gujarat Plains. It borders Gandhinagar, Kheda, Mehsana, and Surendranagar districts. Climate is semi-arid with hot summers, moderate winters, and monsoon rains. Geological features include alluvial soil ideal for agriculture, river valleys, and urban expansions. Statistics and Demographics

According to the 2011 census, Ahmedabad district had a population of 7,214,225, with a density of 890/km², sex ratio of 904 females per 1000 males, and literacy rate of 85.31%. Urban population is predominant at 84.04%. Scheduled Castes 10.54%, Scheduled Tribes 1.24%. Religions: Hinduism 83.6%, Islam 12.24%, Jainism 2.96%. Languages: Gujarati predominant. 2023 estimates show metro population over 8.7 million. Administrative: 12 talukas, 474 villages, 7 municipalities. Economy driven by industries, services. Tourist Spots

Sabarmati Ashram: Gandhi's residence, key independence site. Adalaj Stepwell: Intricate 15th-century architecture. Kankaria Lake: Revamped lakefront with zoo, amusement. Jama Masjid: Grand 15th-century mosque. Hutheesing Jain Temple: Ornate 19th-century marble temple. Sarkhej Roza: Sufi saint's tomb complex. Sidi Saiyyed Mosque: Famous jali screen. Akshardham Temple: Modern Swaminarayan complex. Auto World Vintage Car Museum: Rare vehicles collection. Riverfront: Sabarmati promenade. Other small spots: Law Garden markets, Vastrapur Lake, Thol Lake Bird Sanctuary, heritage walks in pols.
